Projects

Tesla Sustainability Report

Through researching transportation sustainability efforts, I developed a paper that commented on the sustainabiity of Tesla's operations and of their recent Model S launch. With an emphasis on research from the books The Story of Stuff and Cradle to Cradle, I documented both the points of progress and lag.

Time Warner Inc. Investment Thesis

A team and I created a comprehensive investment thesis for the company. We analyzed financial indicators such as price to earnings, debt to equity, book value, EBITDA/EV, and others while researching comps, Time Warner divestment, and historical performance.

Augmented Weighted Undirected Graph

Using hash tables, linked lists, arrays, and protected classes, I created an augmented data structure that emphasized runtime efficiency. We then utilized array-based disjoint sets to program Kruskal's algorithm for finding the minimum spanning tree.

Networks: Artificial Intelligence

Utilizing minimax, alpha-beta pruning, depth first search, and an evaluation function, my partner and I developed a well encapsulated AI that plays Networks. The game is played with two players each with 10 tiles. Both players try to establish a connection of 6 tiles from one end to the other.

Image Edge Detection

Using run-length encoding and pixel transformations, I developed Sobel's algorithm. Through the Run-Length encoding, I was able to reduce the amount of memory usage by consildate consecutive similar pixel into one data point. With all of this, I was able to convert colored photos to grayscale.

Scheme Language Interpreter

My partner and I developed an interpreter for the Scheme language. We parsed the input by reading bracket orientation, and then applied mutual recursion between an eval function and an apply function. Eval determined what the input was, and apply carried out the procedures of that input.

Twitter Feed Demography

By parsing and analyzing Twitter feed data from a single day, I developed a program that was able to determine which states in the U.S. a specific sequence of words were said most often. As an example, the words Obama were documented mostly in predominantly left and predominantly right states.
